Uluk is an MS candidate in Epidemiology and Clinical Research. At Stanford, he is in the Luby Lab and Sarnquist Lab, and Stanford Existential Risks Initiative (SERI) Fellow. He holds a Master of Development Policy (data science concentration) and a Bachelor's in Psychology and Human Rights. His work spans global health research, international human rights policy, and cross-national advocacy across Eastern Europe, Central Asia, Asia-Pacific and Sub-Saharan Africa regions. He has served as a technical expert for WHO's Technical Working Group on School Health Services and as a consultant for UNFPA. He is on the Expert Committee of the French Ministry for Europe and Foreign Affairs' Laboratory for Women's Rights Online, evaluating global project proposals to address technology-facilitated gender-based violence (TFGBV). He has served in board and advisory roles at several international NGOs, including Vice Chair of the IPPF Board of Trustees and Vice Chair of the Global Fund's against HIV, TB and Malaria Youth Council
