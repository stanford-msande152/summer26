**Office hours:** Tue/Thu after lecture, after lunchtime · Huang 261

John Mark Agosta holds Lecturer positions at Stanford, teaching Decision Analysis, and at San José State in Engineering Extended Studies. He left full-time employment after eight years with Microsoft, where he was part of the Azure Data AI Co-innovation Engineering Team. His background in applied research in the Bay Area extends back over 30 years to the early days of AI, with positions at SRI International, Toyota ITC, and Intel Research, interspersed with several startups. He has written over 30 peer-reviewed publications and holds 6 patents. He founded the Bayesian Applications Workshop in 2002 accompanying the Uncertainty in AI conference, and was awarded a Business Fellowship at the Santa Fe Institute in 2007. He holds a B.S. in Physics from Yale University, an M.S. in Science Policy from George Washington University, and a Ph.D. in Management Science and Engineering from Stanford University.
